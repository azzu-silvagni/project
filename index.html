Project Overview

This project explores the intersection of cultural heritage and semantic technologies through the case of Caterina Sforza, one of the most emblematic figures of the Italian Renaissance.

We focus on two fortresses historically linked to her rule — the Rocca di Imola and the Rocca di Ravaldino — with the aim of enriching the ArCo Knowledge Graph.

Using SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs), we retrieved, verified, and generated historical information, which was then formalized into new RDF triples.

This website presents the methodology, the discoveries, and the semantic contributions made to enhance the visibility and accessibility of cultural data in linked open formats.
